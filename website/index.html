Accessibility

About maps and accessibility

A map can be an impassible barrier. During the Covid-19 pandemic, blind people were blocked from booking vaccine appointments because a website required users to interact with a map to move forward in the process. As cartographers, designers, and developers, we can do better.

One rule of thumb is to think of a map as a supplement to more accessible forms of information and interaction. Offer data in other formats, such as tables and lists. Design workflows that don’t depend on using a map to get to the next step. If the map disappeared, could you still complete your task?

TextureMap can make your map work better for people with color vision deficiency, but we want to be clear that it isn’t a complete solution. For a truly accessible experience, provide an alternative option.
